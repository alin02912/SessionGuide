TRUST model

  • Discover the student’s AGN.
    • Consult the intake form
    • Talk with the ISSP
    • Talk with the classroom teacher
    • Ask the students/parents
  • Leverage your understanding of psychometrics
    • GORT = L. Comprehension
    • KTEA = Decoding
    • PPVT = Receptive Vocabulary
  • Discover the student’s interests
    • (virtual) look at student’s profile picture, device background
    • (in-person) look at their clothes, makeup, accessories that they are wearing
    • Ask about their favourite hobbies
    • Ask about classes they are taking
    • Ask about classes they want to take
    • What are their favourite subjects in school
    • Future goals in life
    • Inquire about siblings
  • Build a rapport with the student
    • Demonstrate interest in what the student says
    • Talk about your own interests
    • Connect them to the student’s
    • Follow up in the next sessions
  • Incorporate student’s interests into the instructional content
    • if interested in games, write
    • Talk with the ISSP
    • Talk with the classroom teacher
    • Ask the students/parents
  • Use analogies, if possible, to explain AT
    • Example: R&W's Check-it is your advisor, it gives options but you have to make final decision
  • Demonstrate real world application by working on student assignment
    • AT benefit is most convincing when used on actual school assignments
    • Ask classroom teachers to provide you with the assignments the student is wokring on
  • Match the AGN with the Instructional strategies
  • Common strategies are:
    • TOWER = writing
    • Store and Share = organization
    • SQ4R = reading
    • BUCKS = math
  • Match the AGN with the Instructional strategies
  • Tech is very diverse but can be grouped in clusters
    • TTS, STT, Prediction - Writing
    • TTS, Dict, P. Dict, WS - Comprehension
    • STT, Mindomo/Jamboard, SS - Organization
